Food for thought by the Nobel laureate on the decoding of ribosome structure, Professor Ada Yonath

X-ray crystallography is an accepted technique for examining the spatial structure of biological molecules and other microscopic structures. In this method, crystals are formed from the material, irradiated by X-rays, and from the dispersion of radiation the structure of the molecule is determined. The application of the method to the ribosomes was considered difficult and even impossible due to their size, internal complexity, flexibility and instability, which made it harder to crystallize them into crystals and to perform crystallographic measurements. Yonath did not shy away from the difficulties. She got inspired by bears, the bears, who enter a winter coma, during which the ribosomes in their cells converge into a crystal-like structure.

This is what we mean by information and knowledge, curiosity and freedom to play and explore new directions.
